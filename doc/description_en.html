What does it do?

SOLAR STATION Live has one job: to give you instant feedback on whether your PV system is doing what you expect – without your phone, without logging into an app and without digging through charts.

Energy flow A clear flow diagram shows how much power comes from the panels, what the house is using, how the battery is charging or discharging and whether power is being imported from or exported to the grid.
You can tell at a glance whether you’re running on your own solar production – or on grid power.
Today’s stats Clean numbers for today: generated, consumed, imported, exported. You see how much your PV has “earned” you today and how much of your usage came from your own roof.
Perfect for a quick look in the evening – did the sun pay off today?
24h graph A 24-hour history of generation, consumption and battery state. You see the morning ramp-up, the midday peak and the evening run-down from the battery.
Night-time “power hogs” or load spikes that could be shifted are very easy to spot.
Spot prices A spot electricity price chart for the current day. Expensive and cheap hours are clearly visible – you instantly see whether it’s a good time to run the dryer, dishwasher or charge your EV.
Together with your generation curve it makes planning consumption easy, so your electricity bill stays under control.

“If you don’t have to configure it, you can’t misconfigure it.”

That’s exactly how SOLAR STATION Live behaves. After power-up it automatically looks for the WiFi network of your inverter – if needed, you enter the password once. From then on the station takes care of the connection itself.

If no suitable network is found, it shows a clear list of available inverter networks – just like in the manual. No MAC addresses, no manual IP setup.

Installation

Commissioning is intentionally kept simple, so anyone can do it – no need for network skills, IP addresses or Modbus knowledge.

  1. Place the station where you can see it easily (living room, kitchen, hallway, office).
  2. Plug it into a socket using the supplied USB-C cable and power adapter.
  3. SOLAR STATION Live automatically searches for your inverter’s WiFi and connects.

If something doesn’t work on the first try, the step-by-step “Troubleshooting” guide walks you through typical cases like “WiFi not found” or “no data shown”.

Before you buy

For SOLAR STATION Live to work correctly, the WiFi signal from your inverter must reach the place where you want to put the station. That’s all – no internet access, router or extra hardware is strictly required.

New: The station can now also communicate via your home WiFi. This means better range, more stable connection and more freedom where you place the display in the house.

We currently support typical home installations with one hybrid inverter and one battery. More complex multi-inverter setups are not yet supported – if you’re unsure, just ask us.

Firmware updates

SOLAR STATION Live does not require regular updates – if it works correctly today, it will keep working like that for years.

When new features arrive (such as spot price charts or support for new inverters), you can update the firmware yourself in just a few minutes.

Simply connect the station to your computer via USB-C, open the update page in your browser and follow the instructions. No special software, no deep IT skills – the process is explained step by step.

SOLAR PLUG

SOLAR PLUG sockets and modules help you use surplus energy for useful loads – water heating, space heating, delayed drying, or charging e-bikes and tools.

Once plugged in, the sockets are automatically detected by SOLAR STATION Live and appear as tiles on the screen, including status and real-time power.

Control follows smart rules: it takes PV output, battery level and house consumption into account. The goal is to use as much of your solar energy on-site as possible instead of exporting it cheaply or draining the battery unnecessarily.

EcoVolter Pro

The portable EcoVolter Pro II wallbox (3×16 A / 11 kW) is the ideal companion for a home PV system – it lets you charge your EV from a three-phase socket while respecting the limits of your grid connection.

SOLAR STATION Live shows the EcoVolter as a tile with a charging cable icon, including whether it’s currently charging, at what power and how much it loads your house.

Charging power can be dynamically controlled based on current PV output and household consumption – so your car is charged mainly from surplus solar and grid import is minimized. A perfect combo if you want to drive on sunshine.
